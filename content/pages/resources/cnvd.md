The **Chinese Nonverbal Vocalization Database (CNVD)** comprises recordings from
**50 speakers** portraying **32 nonverbal vocalizations** at medium and high
intensity levels.

Emotions covered are Adoration (喜爱), Amusement (好笑), Anger (气愤), Awe
(令人惊叹), Confusion (困惑), Contempt (轻蔑), Contentment (满足), Desire (欲望),
Desire — For Food (食欲), Desire — For Sex (性欲), Disgust (厌恶), Physical
Disgust (恶心), Distress (苦恼), Embarrassment (尴尬), Excitement (兴奋), Fear
(恐惧), Interest (兴趣), Moved (感动), Physical Pain (痛苦), Praise (赞扬), Pride
(自豪/骄傲), Realization (恍然大悟), Relief (解脱), Sadness (悲伤), Sajiao
(撒娇), Shame (羞愧/内疚), Snicker (坏笑), Snigger (偷笑), Surprise (惊讶),
Positive Surprise (惊喜), Sympathy (同情), and Triumph (胜利).

For each stimulus in CNVD, we provide detailed perceptual and acoustic
descriptions, including recognition accuracy, perceived intensity, valence,
arousal, and eGeMAPS acoustic feature values (Eyben et al., 2016). The database
is available on OSF for research purposes only.
